"ARE"

Interactive Simulators

Active Revenue Engine Framework

Reinforced (RLHF), data-driven system design for predictive and viable revenue generation, and a feedback loop for continuous adaptation to market dynamics.

Core Philosophy

"We must always strive to use mathematical models to prove the viability of instinct and discover latent value points that are often obscured by noise."

Author: Ayomide I. Daniels LinkedIn

A Triad-Based System

PHASE I: DEFINE

P1-P2: Strategic Foundation

Define Revenue Target ($R_T$) and set four target Success Indicators (Fully Met to Exception).

PHASE II: SCRUM

P3-P9: Validation & Logistics

Define Market Entry Value Initiative, define ICP stages, run mandatory Go/No-Go Simulation, and set up comms channels.

PHASE III: EXECUTE

P10: Field Operations

Actively engage qualified leads, feeding real-time data into the Transaction Model to dictate ABANDON/REINFORCE/ACCELERATE actions.

Detailed Processes (P1-P10)

PHASE I: DEFINE

P1 Define Revenue Target

Responsible: Management

Establish the specific Revenue Target ($R_T$) and the defined timeframe or duration required to meet that target.

P2 Set Target Indicators

Responsible: Management

Define quantifiable metrics for measuring the success of the revenue target, creating a clear grading system for the engagement's outcome.

  • Fully Met: 100% of the defined revenue target achieved.
  • Partially Met: X% (defined value) of the target achieved.
  • Worse Case Scenario: Y% (defined value) of the target achieved.
  • Exception: 0% of the target achieved (target not met at all).

PHASE II: SCRUM & VALIDATION

P3 Define Market Entry Value Initiative

Responsible: Product, Marketing, BD

Identify what to sell and craft a compelling Market Entry Value Initiative. This initiative is a Problem and Solution Statement designed to resonate with the target market. This step requires significant creativity in defining the pain point and communicating it.

P4 Define Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Responsible: Marketing, Product, BD

Establish the detailed profile of the market audience from which the target value is expected.

P5 Break ICP into 3 Stages

Responsible: Marketing, Product, BD

Categorize the Ideal Customer Profile based on purchase likelihood into three actionable engagement stages:

  • Will Definitely Buy (WDB)
  • Likely to Buy (LTB)
  • Least Likely to Buy (LLB)

P6 Simulations and Testing (The Go/No-Go Gate)

Responsible: Product, Marketing

A mandatory viability check. Requires two key actions:

  • Testing: Rigorous testing of the offering. Contingency: If Testing fails, the entire engagement must be immediately cancelled.
  • Simulation (Transaction Model): Uses historical and publicly available competitor data to predict lead qualification and sales likelihood.

P6 Defining Equation (Expected Revenue $R_E$):

$$\\ R_E = V \cdot \sum_{i \in \{WDB, LTB, LLB\}} (N_i \cdot CR_i) \\$$

Where $V$ is Average Deal Value, $N_i$ is Lead Volume for Stage $i$, and $CR_i$ is Conversion Rate for Stage $i$.

Data Fields for Transaction Model (Historical & Real-Life):

  • Lead History: Past and recent purchases of similar items (competitive items).
  • Financials: Item Cost, Amount Paid, Deal Duration (time to close).
  • Geographics: Item Origin (location) and ICP Preferences for Item Origin.
  • Qualitative: Insider information regarding pain points (need, replacement, or lack of awareness).

MANDATORY CONTINGENCY: If Simulation results do not favor meeting the revenue target (P2), the engagement should be cancelled or immediately reviewed by Management.

P7 Add Justification Checklists

Responsible: Sales, BD

For each ICP stage (P5), define the $\mathbf{J^+}$ (positive) and $\mathbf{J^-}$ (negative) justification checklist. These weighted factors explain why a deal might successfully close or why it might fail.

P8 Leads Generation & Categorization

Responsible: Marketing, Sales

Active generation of new leads and their subsequent categorization into the three defined ICP stages based on criteria established in P5 and P6.

P9 Setup Communication Chain Folder

Responsible: Product, Branding

Establish and organize all necessary communication and tracking channels for this specific revenue period to ensure consistent data flow.

This includes setting up dedicated channels across: CRM, Demo Planning, Message Logging, Feedback Tracking and Analytics, and Papers/Documentation.

PHASE III: EXECUTE & OPTIMIZE

P10 Field Operations & Optimization

Responsible: Sales, Product

Active engagement with qualified leads to sell to them. All field operations comms, feedbacks, and interactions must flow through the channels set up in P9.

P10 Defining Equation (Live Close Probability $P'_{close}$):

$$\\ P'_{close} = CR_{initial} + (\alpha \cdot H_{Deal}) \\$$

Where $CR_{initial}$ is the Conversion Rate from P5, $H_{Deal}$ is the accumulated Deal Health (from $J^+$ and $J^-$ events), and $\alpha$ is the Adaptation Factor (weighting the health score).

Real-Time Decision Model:

Product and Business Development teams must continuously engage a decision model that updates in real-time using field data ($\mathbf{J^+}$ and $\mathbf{J^-}$ events from P7). This model dictates the immediate required action:

  • ACCELERATE (high $\mathbf{P'_{close}}$)
  • REINFORCE (medium $\mathbf{P'_{close}}$)
  • ABANDON/REVIEW (low $\mathbf{P'_{close}}$)
